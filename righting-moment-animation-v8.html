Why two catamarans look so different. A performance catamaran designed for offshore sailing — wider beam relative to length, shorter rig, lower bridgedeck, lower centre of gravity — builds a much larger righting arm before reaching its peak. The Nahoa M's GZmax sits roughly 75% higher than a typical production cruising cat of similar length, and stays well above the ISO 12215-5 inversion-resistance threshold with real margin. A charter-style cruising cat with tall freeboard, a flybridge, and a taller rig pushes its centre of gravity up and its sail forces higher — which shrinks the righting arm at every angle of heel and brings GZmax down closer to the line.
