Catamaran heel behaviour

At rest, both hulls float with a significant portion submerged — the waterline cuts through each hull. As heel increases, the boat rotates about the leeward hull; the windward hull lifts until its bottom just clears the waterline at GZmax (≈14°). That moment is the maximum righting lever the boat can generate. Past it, nothing left to push back.

How to read it. The catamaran curve climbs steeply and peaks near 14°. That peak is GZmax — the maximum righting lever the boat can ever generate, and the value ISO 12215-5 evaluates for inversion resistance. Past the peak the curve declines gradually and eventually crosses zero. The monohull curve builds slowly, peaks near 60–65°, and stays positive well past 90° — a keelboat is actively fighting capsize the whole way through a knockdown.

Catamaran curve approximates Archi-Delion's 15 April 2026 data. Monohull curve is a representative ballasted keelboat shape. Use Archi-Delion's official curves for any technical publication.
